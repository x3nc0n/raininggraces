# Future Blog Post Ideas

Ideas for upcoming posts on the Raining Graces blog. Each entry includes a
working title, the content pillar it falls under (see blog-style-guide.md),
and a brief outline of what the post would cover.

---

## 1. What Does a Birth Doula Actually Do?

**Pillar:** Education & evidence
**Tags:** `doula`, `education`, `birth`

Demystify the doula role for families who are curious but unsure. Cover what a
doula does (and doesn't do) before, during, and after labor. Address common
misconceptions — "Will she replace my partner?" "Is this only for unmedicated
births?" Close with Robin's personal approach and what makes her support unique.

---

## 2. Your Birth Partner Is Your MVP — Here's How a Doula Helps Them Shine

**Pillar:** Empowerment & encouragement
**Tags:** `doula`, `partners`, `support`

Speak directly to partners (dads, spouses, loved ones). Explain how doula
support amplifies — not replaces — the birth partner's role. Include practical
tips partners can use in labor and how Robin coaches them in real time.

---

## 3. What to Expect at a Spinning Babies® Birth Prep Class

**Pillar:** Education & evidence
**Tags:** `spinning-babies`, `education`, `class`

Walk parents through what a class looks like — the Three Balances℠, daily
activities, partner involvement, and the "why" behind each technique. Help
hesitant families see the class as approachable and empowering, not clinical.

---

## 4. Creating a Birth Preference Sheet That Actually Works

**Pillar:** Empowerment & encouragement
**Tags:** `birth-plan`, `preparation`, `education`

Guide parents through building a flexible, practical birth preference sheet.
Cover what to include, what to skip, how to communicate it to your care team,
and why the word "preferences" matters more than "plan."

## 5. When Birth Doesn't Go as Planned — Finding Peace in the Unexpected

**Pillar:** Personal story & calling
**Tags:** `doula`, `faith`, `support`

A gentle, compassionate post about navigating birth when things shift — longer
labor, unexpected interventions, cesarean birth. Normalize the emotions, affirm
that support still matters, and share how Robin holds space for families in
those moments. Weave in faith-centered encouragement.
